How I'm reading this

design reasoning

The image does a lot of work already: a chrome chess rook floating in ashy near-black, with “LOCK IN” set in blackletter. That's a strong, premium identity — heavy, focused, a little defiant. My job is to build a page that feels like an extension of that frame, not a busier version of it.

So the strategy is restraint. Lots of black. Generous negative space. One material — polished silver — used like a precious metal, not a paint. Type is clean and quiet so the chrome and the rook stay the loudest things on screen. The whole page should feel like one continuous dark room you scroll through, where light catches metal here and there.

The hero image is small (736×414), so I'll treat it as a contained centerpiece framed in darkness rather than a stretched full-bleed background — that keeps it crisp and makes the rook feel like an object on a pedestal.

Big idea: “Lock In” = the ritual of focus. The site sells a feeling — closing the door on the noise — more than a feature list.
